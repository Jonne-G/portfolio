Build almost from scratch, the Remedy Houdini-environment is the main way of interacting with Houdini at Remedy.
For most of its development I was the sole developer responsible for building and maintaining an environment that would allow for easy access to Houdini; management of HDAs (Houdini Digital Assets) on multiple projects; as well as making said HDAs easy to create and update.
Over the various iterations the "backend" went through,
the current version has three main components:

1. Houdini launch process

When the user clicks one of the shortcuts (Houdini core, FX, and Apprentice) the sequence shown above is triggered creating a scene where the user can use Houdini without worrying about the details.

2. i/o tooling

Getting files into and out of a DCC is one of the most important parts of a pipeline.
As such, the pipeline features a collection of nodes to roundtrip not just geometry files (namely FBX), but also proprietary metadata used in the Northlight engine.

3. Remedy Shelf

To give users easy access to all important operation, the decision was made to put them in plain sight. As such, all the important items like: creating, updating, or submitting a HDA are all in a single place alongside a collection of other common utility functions
